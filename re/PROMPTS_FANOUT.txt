================================================================================
MASHED RE — PARALLEL FANOUT PROMPTS
================================================================================

Nine self-contained prompts: A–F are Sonnet analysis sessions (run in parallel,
each takes its own pool slot, queues for sweep instead of writing master itself),
G–H are no-MCP file-format sessions (no Ghidra contention), and I is the single
Opus sweep that drains everyone's queued work under one master claim.

Recommended launch order:
  Wave 1: A, B, C  (boot/CRT — proves protocol with overlapping subsystems)
  Wave 2: D, E, F  (RW init, RW teardown, RWS audio — different subsystems)
  Anytime: G, H    (no Ghidra needed; can overlap with any wave)
  Last:    I       (only after A–F have all pushed their analysis + queue rows)

You can launch all 8 (A–H) simultaneously if you trust the queue protocol — RVA
subsets are mathematically disjoint by anchor strategy. Wave-of-3 is the
debug-friendly default.

Reference docs every prompt assumes are present and committed:
  - CLAUDE.md
  - re/SESSION_RULES.md (especially § "Parallel-fanout scribe-queue pattern"
    and § "ID range allocation")
  - re/SCRIBE_QUEUE.md (the queue file)
  - .claude/skills/ghidra-pool/SKILL.md
  - .claude/skills/multi-session/SKILL.md
