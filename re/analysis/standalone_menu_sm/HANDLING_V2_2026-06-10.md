# R6 handling v2 — what is PORTED-shape vs SCAFFOLD (2026-06-10)

Source: existing C1/C2 notes, primarily `re/analysis/vehicle_update_d2/00470670.md`
(VehicleControlUpdate, 0x00470670). The original's car-state struct (offsets from
the vehicle object base, all cited in that note):

| offset | meaning | adopted in standalone model |
|---|---|---|
| +0x9b0..0x9b8 | velocity vec3 | YES — `car_vel_[3]` replaces the scalar speed |
| +0x9d4..0x9dc | forward vec3 | YES — derived from yaw |
| +0x190 | per-vehicle max-speed constant | shape adopted (tuning scaffold) |
| +0x1a8 / +0x26c | throttle force accumulators (force along forward) | YES — throttle applies force along forward |
| +0xb0c | `(dt − |dot(fwd,vel)|/speed)·speed` = NON-forward (lateral/slide) speed measure | YES — grip model bleeds the lateral velocity component |
| +0xb24/+0xb28 | boost timers | not yet (no boost input) |

ADOPTED (struct + update shape, from the decomp): velocity-vector state; throttle
as a force along forward; brake/reverse as the negated pipeline; slide measured as
the velocity component orthogonal to forward; per-tick integration with delta-time
scaling.

## Telemetry harvest (2026-06-10, R6-faithful) — `harvest_handling.py`

Drove the ORIGINAL to a Quick Battle round and Interceptor-sampled all 4 cars'
structs at VehicleControlUpdate (0x00470670), 20k samples
(`log/handling_telemetry.jsonl`). Findings:
- `+0x190` max-speed constant = **34.0**, rock-solid across all 4 cars. It is
  in the original's INTERNAL units — observed velocity magnitudes (`+0x9b0`)
  top out ~4200, a ~120× internal scale — so the standalone keeps `kTop`
  track-radius-relative rather than copying 34.0.
- **Coast decay k ≈ 0.21/s** (time-constant ~4.7s) — fitted from throttle-off
  segments. This RATE is unit-independent and transfers directly:
  `kDrag = 0.21`. The cars are heavy/weighty, not arcade-twitchy.
- Lateral slide (`+0xb0c` measure) rms ≈ 2% of forward speed → strong grip;
  `kGrip = 6.0`.
- `kThrottle = kTop·0.42` gives a natural top of 2×kTop (clamped) and a ~3.3s
  spool-up — matching the harvested weighty feel.
Caveat: my menu input-override (FUN_00497310) does NOT reach the in-race car,
so the moving samples are the self-driving AI; per-axis throttle separation and
a full input-matched diff remain DEFERRED (needs an in-race input injector).
